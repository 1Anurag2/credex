# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Target User
The exact target user is a **VP of Engineering**, **CTO**, or **Lead Engineer** at a Series A/B startup (20-150 employees). These individuals are managing rising cloud/SaaS bills, hold the budget for developer tools, and are actively feeling the pain of tool sprawl as individual contributors expense Cursor, Copilot, ChatGPT Plus, and Claude Pro haphazardly. 

## Pre-Intent Context
Right before they'd want this tool, they are:
- Scrolling through their monthly Ramp or Brex statement, sighing at the dozens of scattered $20 charges for various AI tools.
- Searching Reddit (`r/startups`, `r/ExperiencedDevs`) or Hacker News for "Cursor vs Copilot vs Claude enterprise pricing".
- Trying to build a spreadsheet to figure out if it's cheaper to buy an Enterprise seat or let devs expense individual Pro accounts.

## Where They Hang Out
- **Subreddits**: `r/ExperiencedDevs`, `r/SaaS`, `r/startups`
- **Slack/Discord**: CTO Craft, Rands Leadership Slack, Y Combinator Alumni Slack.
- **X (Twitter)**: Following engineering leaders like Gergely Orosz (Pragmatic Engineer), swyx.

## Getting the First 100 Users ($0 Paid Budget)
1. **The "Engineering Audit" Playbook**: I will write a high-value technical blog post titled *"How we cut our AI dev tool spend by 40% without taking away anyone's Cursor"*. This post will break down the exact math (e.g., when to switch from individual seats to Team, how API usage compares to Plus subscriptions). At the bottom, I will embed the Audit Tool as a free calculator.
2. **Community Injection**: I will drop the tool in Rands Leadership Slack (#engineering-managers channel) on a Tuesday morning with a message like: *"Hey all, noticed a lot of teams struggling with fragmented AI tool spend. Built a quick calculator that runs the math on Cursor vs Copilot vs Claude enterprise tiers. Found out we were overpaying by $4k/yr. Thought it might be useful here."*
3. **Cold Outreach to "CFOs of Startups"**: I will search LinkedIn for "Fractional CFO" or "Head of Finance" at tech startups and send a direct, personalized message: *"Noticed you handle finance for [Company]. Devs expense a lot of AI tools right now. Built a free calculator to see if your engineering team is overpaying. Usually finds ~$500/mo in savings."*

## The Unfair Distribution Channel
**Credex's Existing Network of Discounted Credits**: The unfair advantage is that the tool doesn't just say "You're overspending." It says, "You're overspending by $1,000/mo, and **we have a private link to get you 30% off Anthropic/Cursor right now**." Other calculators just give advice; this tool actually *provides the discounted inventory*. 

## Week-1 Traction Goals
If this works, Week-1 looks like:
- 1,500 unique visitors (driven by Hacker News/Twitter launch).
- 300 completed audits (20% conversion to complete the form).
- 60 emails captured (20% of completions submit email to view the detailed report).
- 5 high-intent "Consultation Booked" leads for Credex (accounts with >$500/mo savings).
